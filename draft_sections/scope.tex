\subsection{When Does the Framework Help?}
\label{sec:scope}

A useful account of a mechanism states both axes of its scope: the \emph{data} on
which it helps, and the \emph{backbone} into which it is dropped. Our experiments
separate the two cleanly.
On the data axis, MRMA helps precisely when the training split admits three
\emph{complementary} relation graphs that recover a genuine local dependency
structure, and reduces to the backbone when it does not.
The gains concentrate on flow-type traffic sensor networks---all four PEMS
benchmarks---where directed segment coupling, marginal congestion co-movement,
and lead-lag propagation zones are three physically distinct phenomena, so the
partial-correlation, Pearson, and 2D-structural-entropy community graphs each
carry signal the others miss.
On the backbone axis, the masks deliver their full benefit on a single global
channel-attention (\mr{}${=}$\itrans{}${+}$MRMA improves all 16 PEMS cells over
its all-ones control), but only part of it, or none, on backbones that already
impose their own channel structure---5 of 16 cells on CrossFormer's two-stage
router, neutral on a gated state-space recurrence. There the fixed masks compete
with, rather than replace, an existing learned mixing.

Outside this regime the framework neither helps nor materially hurts---it
converges to the backbone---and we report the boundary explicitly rather than
suppressing it.
Three conditions mark the edge.
\emph{(i) Diffuse or single-driver dependency:} when one dominant factor drives
all channels (photovoltaic panels under shared irradiance; finance series such
as exchange-rate and NASDAQ equity data with pervasive factor co-movement), the
three graphs collapse toward the same structure, the relational heads lose their
division of labour, and the masked model tracks full attention within noise.
\emph{(ii) Low channel count:} for small-$N$ data (ETT-family, Weather, with
$N{\lesssim}20$) the top-$k$ neighbourhoods already cover most channel pairs, so
there is little to sparsify and the mask union is near-dense; gains fall within
seed noise.
\emph{(iii) Wrong physical quantity on an otherwise structured network:} even on
large traffic networks, the prior helps only when the recovered communities
correspond to a physically grounded flow structure. On traffic-\emph{speed}
data---and on diffuse Bay-Area-wide loop-detector correlations---the estimated
communities do not align with a local propagation structure, and the fixed prior
removes capacity the backbone would otherwise use, so the masks are neutral to
mildly harmful.
This is the same mechanism read in reverse: the benefit is contingent on
graph--data alignment, so where alignment fails the benefit vanishes with it.

We regard this two-axis scope as a feature of an honest paper, not a caveat to be
minimised.
MRMA is a \emph{structural prior for flow-type sensor networks processed by a
single global channel-attention}: it is exactly as strong as the structure its
three graphs can recover from the training split and as the room the host
backbone leaves for a static mask to fill---no stronger, and, on the datasets or
backbones where either condition fails, no weaker than the backbone it drops
into.
